Mindfulness and Self Care

Guided Meditation:

Here's a simple guided meditation for mindfulness and self-care. You can do this sitting or lying down in a comfortable position. Let's begin. Click the video below to begin the guided meditation

  1. Settle In: Take a deep breath in through your nose, filling your lungs completely. Hold it for a moment, and then exhale slowly through your mouth. Let any tension release with your breath. Repeat this two more times, inhaling deeply… and exhaling fully.
  2. Breathe: Find a comfortable position. Gently close your eyes if you feel comfortable doing so. Take a moment to notice how your body feels against the surface beneath you. Allow yourself to relax.
  3. Focus on Your Breath: Now, allow your breath to return to its natural rhythm. Notice the sensation of the air entering and leaving your body. Feel your chest rise and fall, your abdomen expand and contract. Just observe your breath without trying to change it.
  4. Ground Yourself: Bring your awareness to the points of contact where your body meets the ground or the surface beneath you. Feel the support beneath you. Imagine it grounding you, helping you feel stable and secure.
  5. Check In With Your Body: Now, gently shift your focus to your thoughts. Notice any thoughts that come up, like clouds passing through the sky. Acknowledge them without engaging or judging. Let them drift away, returning your attention to your breath whenever you get distracted.
  6. Cultivate Mindfulness: Now, gently shift your focus to your thoughts. Notice any thoughts that come up, like clouds passing through the sky. Acknowledge them without engaging or judging. Let them drift away, returning your attention to your breath whenever you get distracted.
  7. Self-Care Affirmation: Silently or aloud, repeat a self-care affirmation that resonates with you, such as:
    • "I am worthy of love and care."
    • "I give myself permission to rest."
    • "I am enough, just as I am."
    Feel the truth of these words sink into your being.
  8. Return to the Present: Gradually bring your awareness back to the room. Notice any sounds around you, the sensation of the air on your skin. Wiggle your fingers and toes, gently reawakening your body.
  9. Open Your Eyes: When you’re ready, slowly open your eyes. Take a moment to notice how you feel. Carry this sense of mindfulness and self-care with you as you go about your day.
